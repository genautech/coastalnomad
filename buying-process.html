Everything you need to know about buying property in Brazil

üåé Can foreigners buy property in Brazil?

Yes! Foreigners have the same property rights as Brazilian citizens. You can buy, own, and sell property without restrictions in most areas. Some coastal and border zones have special rules, but I'll guide you through any requirements.

Required documents: Valid passport, Brazilian CPF (tax ID - I'll help you get this), proof of address, proof of funds.

‚úÖ Foreign-friendly

üí∞ What are the total costs involved?

Purchase costs:

  • Property price (varies by location)
  • ITBI Tax: ~2-3% of property value
  • Registration fees: ~0.5-1%
  • Legal fees: $1,500-3,000
  • Real estate commission: Usually paid by seller

Ongoing costs: Property tax (IPTU) ~0.5-1.5% annually, condo fees if applicable.

üíµ Transparent pricing

‚è±Ô∏è How long does the process take?

Typical timeline:

  • Property search: 1-4 weeks
  • Due diligence: 1-2 weeks
  • Negotiation & contract: 1-2 weeks
  • Registration: 2-4 weeks

Total: 6-12 weeks from first contact to keys in hand. Can be faster for cash purchases or slower if financing is involved.

‚ö° 6-12 weeks

üè¶ Can I get financing/mortgage as a foreigner?

Financing options for foreigners are limited but available through some banks. Requirements are stricter:

  • Minimum 30-50% down payment
  • Brazilian bank account and CPF
  • Proof of income (may need Brazilian source)
  • Higher interest rates (8-12% vs 3-5% for locals)

Most foreign buyers purchase with cash or arrange financing in their home country. I can connect you with banks that work with foreigners.

üè¶ Limited options

üìú Do I need a lawyer?

Highly recommended! While not legally required, having a lawyer protects your investment and ensures all documents are in order.

What the lawyer does:

  • Verify property titles and ownership
  • Check for liens, debts, or legal issues
  • Review and explain all contracts
  • Ensure proper registration
  • Represent your interests

I work with bilingual lawyers who specialize in foreign buyers. Costs are $1,500-3,000.

üîí How safe is my investment?

Very safe with proper due diligence! Brazil has a well-established property registration system.

Protection measures:

  • Property titles registered at official registry
  • Lawyer verification of all documents
  • Title insurance available
  • Clear property rights for foreigners
  • Legal recourse through courts

I only work with verified properties and conduct thorough background checks. Your investment is protected by Brazilian law.

üèñÔ∏è Can I rent out my property?

Yes! Both long-term and short-term rentals are allowed and popular.

Rental options:
